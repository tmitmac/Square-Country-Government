2019 Earlington Evacuations, six years on.

December 23, 2025

The following is an excerpt of an article written in 2019 about the Earlington Evacuations

Earlier this month, Square Country faced one of its more unsettling natural scares when melting snow and ice on the Quint-Peaks raised serious concerns about flooding in Earlington, the nationâ€™s former capital.

As temperatures rose and runoff increased, officials warned that the river systems feeding into the village could overflow with little notice. With forecasts uncertain and the risk growing by the hour, Prime Minister Ian Feld authorised a full evacuation of Earlington. Residents were moved to nearby towns and temporary accommodation, while emergency services and engineers monitored water levels and prepared flood defences.

The evacuation quickly became a national talking point. Images of empty streets and closed historic buildings circulated widely, and many questioned how close the country was to losing one of its most important settlements. Despite the anxiety, authorities maintained that the decision was made out of caution, given how unpredictable alpine meltwater can be.

Ultimately, the worst did not occur. Water levels peaked below critical thresholds, and no damage was recorded in the village. Within days, residents were allowed to return home, relieved but aware of how narrowly disaster had been avoided.

In the aftermath, the Feld government moved to strengthen protections around Earlington. New flood monitoring systems were installed, and existing barriers were reinforced, with officials citing the 2019 evacuation as a clear warning. What began as a frightening episode ended as a reminder of the need for preparedness when nature shows its hand.

So, what's happened since then?

Earlington has come close to a similar event twice. Once in 2021, and again in 2023. However, the monitoring systems commissioned by the Feld administration proved more than effective against melting glacial ice.

Whilst the Prime Minister, Ryan Parker, offers warm thanks to the first responders who acted heroically on the day, Leader of the Opposition, UNDETERMINED, criticised the Liberty-led Goverment. "The government sees this as a problem to be managed. I see it as an issue to be fixed. Climate change is damaging communities. We need to act now."

Experts assure Earlingtonians that any similar event wouldn't happen without ample warning time, and safety systems would prevent any damage to the historic town.
