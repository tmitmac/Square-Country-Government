Prime Minister Ryan Parker Sets Election Date, Unveils Northern Prosperity Plan

February 3, 2026

Prime Minister Ryan Parker has confirmed that Square Country will head to the polls on 17 October, formally launching the election year with the release of the government’s first major policy: the Northern Prosperity Plan.

The plan is aimed squarely at the Northern Territories, with the government promising to supercharge economic growth, attract private investment, and create tens of thousands of well-paying jobs — without relying on what Parker described as “blank-cheque handouts.”

Making the North a Magnet for Investment

A central feature of the policy is the creation of Northern Investment Zones (NIZs), which will designate key urban centres, industrial hubs, and emerging tech clusters as priority development areas. Businesses operating in these zones will receive a 50% reduction in corporate tax for their first five years, along with additional deductions for hiring local workers.

Planning approvals for manufacturing, technology, and clean-energy projects will also be fast-tracked, with the government aiming to cut red tape and reduce approval times by up to 40 percent.

Infrastructure and Skills at the Core

The Northern Prosperity Plan commits to a major infrastructure push, including upgrades to regional roads and highways, modernisation of ports to support exports, and expanded high-speed broadband across northern towns and cities. Many projects will be delivered through public-private partnerships, combining private capital with government oversight.

Alongside infrastructure, the government will roll out a Northern Skills & Jobs Program, funding apprenticeships, technical training, and upskilling in high-demand sectors such as manufacturing, clean energy, IT, and logistics. Northern Skills Hubs will be established to connect training directly with local job placements.

Support for Local Industry and Agriculture

Local industries — including agriculture, forestry, tourism, and technology startups — will receive targeted support through grants, low-interest loans, and export assistance. A new Northern Export Accelerator Program will help northern businesses expand into international markets, while innovation hubs will encourage collaboration between universities and industry.

The government estimates the plan will cost between $1.8 and $2.5 billion over five years, funded through reallocated spending, infrastructure bonds, and private-sector partnerships.

Greens Push Back

Green Party leader Erin Starling criticised the announcement, warning that the plan risks prioritising growth over environmental protection in the Northern Territories.

“This plan puts tax breaks and fast-tracked approvals ahead of environmental safeguards,” Starling said. “Without stronger protections, large-scale development could damage fragile northern ecosystems and undermine long-term sustainability.”

Election Battle Lines Drawn

With the election date now locked in, analysts say the Northern Prosperity Plan will form a central plank of Liberty’s campaign. While the party currently governs with a majority, recent polling suggests October’s vote could result in a more fragmented parliament — making regional development policies a key battleground for undecided voters.
